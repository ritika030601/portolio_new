Food Rescue Network

02. MingleMeal

MingleMeal connects restaurants, volunteers, and NGOs to simplify food rescue logistics and cut down on waste through a role-based product flow built around clarity and speed.

01 / Context

The problem with food rescue

Every evening, restaurants throw away food that was prepared but not sold. Meanwhile, NGOs that feed communities are often one step behind - they find out about donations too late, cannot arrange pickup in time, and the food is already gone. The infrastructure for redistribution exists: restaurants, drivers, and NGOs are all willing to participate. The coordination is what breaks down.

After talking to people across all three user groups, it became clear that the problem was not motivation - it was timing and visibility. Restaurants did not know who to notify. Drivers did not know where to go or what they were picking up. NGOs did not know what was coming or when. Everyone had different definitions of what "working" means.

The goal became designing one shared system that could speak three different languages - and still get the food where it needed to go.

02 / Research

What I found when I listened

I started by talking to each user group separately, before I touched a single wireframe. What became clear immediately was that each group had a completely different mental model of the problem - and any design that tried to solve all three from the same perspective would fail all three.

Restaurants wanted to donate but dreaded the admin. They needed a single tap - log what is available, set a window, done. Anything more was a barrier.
Drivers needed clarity above all else. What am I picking up, where exactly, and when? Ambiguity meant delays, and delays meant food going cold.
NGOs needed predictability. They could plan around a confirmed pickup, but not around a "maybe." Real-time status updates were not a nice-to-have - they were essential.

03 / Solution

One system, three flows

The design answer was a single shared platform with role-specific entry points. Each user type sees only what they need - nothing more, nothing less - but every action they take updates the same underlying state.

Restaurants log a donation in under a minute. The app prompts for food type, quantity, and pickup window - and then handles the rest.
Drivers receive a pickup brief with address, food details, and delivery contact. QR verification at pickup and drop-off creates a clear handoff record.
NGOs see live status for incoming food - from logged to in transit to delivered. No more waiting by the phone. No more uncertainty about whether food is actually on the way.
