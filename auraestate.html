01 / Context

When one flow serves no one well

Real estate platforms typically build a single experience and ask both investors and buyers to navigate it. The problem is that these two groups want entirely different things. Investors care about yield, market data, and portfolio views. Buyers care about location, space, lifestyle fit, and trust signals. Giving them the same interface means both groups spend time filtering out what does not apply to them.

The research confirmed what common sense suggests: both user types drop off early when the platform does not immediately reflect their intent. The question was whether one app could serve both - or whether it needed to be two apps with a shared front door.

The answer turned out to be neither extreme. A single entry point with a clear, low-friction mode selection could set the right context immediately, and the rest of the experience could branch from there without feeling disconnected.

02 / Research

Two personas, two completely different journeys

Research centred on dual-persona interviews and comparative journey mapping to understand where the experience broke down for each group - and what they needed to feel confident moving forward.

Investors wanted data-first. They needed yield estimates, price history, and neighbourhood comparisons front and centre. Lifestyle photography felt like noise to them.
Buyers wanted to feel something. They responded to visual storytelling, neighbourhood character, and clear information about the buying process - not raw numbers.
Both groups shared one expectation: the platform had to earn their trust quickly. High-intent journeys - whether financial or personal - require confidence before commitment.

03 / Solution

One door, two experiences

A simple, prominent mode toggle at entry - Investor or Buyer - sets the context for everything that follows. No long onboarding, no questionnaire. One choice, instantly applied.
Investor mode surfaces market analytics, projected returns, and portfolio tools as the primary content. Property photos move to secondary position.
Buyer mode leads with photography, neighbourhood stories, and lifestyle context. Financial data is available but not pushed. The emotional side of the decision is respected.
The brand stays consistent across both modes - same colours, same typography, same trust signals - so switching feels like a preference, not a different product.
