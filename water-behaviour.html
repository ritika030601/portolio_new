Case Study - Consumer Behavior Research

01. Water Behaviour Research

A behavioral study that explains why users reach for a new product instead of an available one. The case study focuses on decision architecture, visibility, and product patterns that make reuse the obvious choice.

View full case study on Behance

The Problem

Users frequently disregard existing choices and prefer a new option because the interface fails to make the available resource feel intentional and trustworthy.

Existing options are hidden behind unfamiliar presentation.
Freshness bias makes new products feel safer.
Decision friction pushes users toward the easier, default path.

The Solution

Surface available choices first with high visual priority.
Use clear validation cues to build trust in reuse.
Position reuse as the recommended and low-friction action.

Research Focus

The research combined observation, cognitive testing, and interviews to understand why users default to a new product even when one is already available.

Observed 50+ natural decision moments in real use.
Measured choice speed and perceived confidence.
Interviewed users about trust cues and freshness bias.
