Fitness and Habit UX

03. Avptivo

Avptivo is a wellness ecosystem designed to strengthen workout consistency, nutrition clarity, and long-term habit retention - by connecting all three into one daily loop instead of three separate experiences.

01 / Context

Why people stop showing up

Most fitness apps are actually three separate apps stitched together - a workout tracker, a meal logger, and a progress dashboard. Users switch between them, lose context, and eventually stop using all of them. The experience is fragmented by design, even when the intention is not.

Avptivo started from one question: what if the app knew what you did today, adjusted your meal suggestion accordingly, and showed you one clear next step - without you having to piece it together yourself? That is the experience this project set out to build.

The research made clear that habit formation breaks down not from lack of motivation, but from unclear next steps. People drop off at the moment when the app asks them to make a decision instead of making it for them.

02 / Research

What users actually said

Research was grounded in habit psychology and real user behaviour around exercise, nutrition, and daily routine. I interviewed 15 people across different fitness levels and tracked where they dropped off in existing apps.

Users skipped workouts most often not because they lacked time, but because the decision of what to do next felt too open-ended. They wanted to be told, not asked.
Nutrition logging was the first thing to go when motivation dipped. People found it tedious and disconnected from the workout experience - it felt like admin, not part of their routine.
Progress tracking was motivating only when visible in context. A streak or badge buried in a separate tab did nothing. Progress shown right after completing a session - did a lot.

03 / Solution

A daily loop, not three separate apps

The home screen always shows one clear action: the next thing in the user's daily loop. Not a menu of choices - just the next step.
Meal suggestions are generated after workout completion, based on what the user just did. This ties nutrition to effort in a way that feels relevant, not random.
Progress feedback appears inline - immediately after each completed session. Streaks, weekly totals, and body markers are visible without navigating away.
